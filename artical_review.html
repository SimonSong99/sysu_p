A novel probability-based diagnostic imaging with weight compensation for damage localization using guided waves

Kehai Liu, Shuyi Ma, Zhanjun Wu, Yuebin Zheng, Xiaoxi Qu, Yishou Wang, Wenhua Wu

SHM 2016


Review

  The paper introduced a weight-compensated probability-based diagnostic imaging (WCPDI) approach to improve the accuracy of damage localization in structural health monitoring (SHM). By proposing a new unit weight distribution to estimate the elliptical distribution of weight, the influence of weight distribution dominance was reduced, and thus the precision of damage localization of the WCPDI algorithm was significantly improved compared to the conventional PDI algorithm.

Probability-based diagnostic imaging using hybrid features extracted from ultrasonic Lamb wave signals

Chao Zhou, Zhongqing Su, Li Cheng

SMS 2011


Review

The paper presents a retrofitted damage identification approach using Lamb wave signals and a hybrid image fusion scheme. The approach combines time-of-flight, signal energy intensity, and signal correlation features to quantitatively visualize structural damage. The effectiveness of the approach is demonstrated through the identification of various damage cases.

Modified Lamb Wave Delay Factor-Based Multiply–Sum Probabilistic Diagnostic Imaging Algorithm of Composite Structures

Lingyu Sun , Juntao Wei , Chang Peng , Zengye Ju , Shanshan Lv , Faye Zhang , Lei Zhang , Lei Jia , and Mingshun Jiang

IEEE 2023


Review

  Probabilistic diagnostic imaging (PDI) method has been confirmed to be promising in structural health monitoring (SHM). The imaging capability of PDI method is limited by numerous factors, including the selection of probability distribution function and ellipsoidal scaling factor, the density of sensing arrays, and the damage index.
  The author proposed a modifed delay-factor-basde multiply-sum PDI (MDF-MSPDI) algorithm. In which the author obtains a more accurate delay factor (DF) by improving the culculation method of the group velocity of wave, and therefore the independence of gloabal accuracy on the selection of scaling factor β was reduced. At the same time, this study proposed an multiply–sum (MS) operation to reconstruct the damage imaging of each path, and generally reduce the dependence on individual paths, which can greatly improve the imaging accuracy of PDI method.

Baseline-Free Damage Diagnostic Imaging Approach Relying on the Extraction of Converted Modes of Ultrasonic Guided Waves

Jiaqi Zhang, Hao Xu; Kai Zhou, Zhengyan Yang, Kehai Liu, Yuebin Zheng, Shuyi Ma, and Zhanjun Wu

ASCE 2021


Review

  A novel converted mode extraction-probabilistic diagnostic imaging (CME-PDI) method, which combined the conventional PDI and the CME methods, was proposed. By reconstructing the bisis mode, a new calculation method of damage index (DI) is presented, rusulting in the independence of the obtainment of baseline data, and therefore the CME-PDI method significantly improves the adapt ability of the traditional PDI method to the change of envirmental temperature.
