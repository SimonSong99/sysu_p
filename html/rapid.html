Probability-based diagnostic imaging(PDI)









NO.1

A novel probability-based diagnostic imaging with weight compensation for damage localization using guided waves

Kehai Liu, Shuyi Ma, Zhanjun Wu, Yuebin Zheng, Xiaoxi Qu, Yishou Wang, and Wenhua Wu

SHM 2016


Review

  The paper introduced a weight-compensated probability-based diagnostic imaging (WCPDI) approach to improve the accuracy of damage localization in structural health monitoring (SHM). By proposing a new unit weight distribution to estimate the elliptical distribution of weight, the influence of weight distribution dominance was reduced, and thus the precision of damage localization of the WCPDI algorithm was significantly improved compared to the conventional PDI algorithm.

NO.2

Probability-based diagnostic imaging using hybrid features extracted from ultrasonic Lamb wave signals

Chao Zhou, Zhongqing Su, and Li Cheng

SMS 2011


Review

The paper presents a retrofitted damage identification approach using Lamb wave signals and a hybrid image fusion scheme. The approach combines time-of-flight, signal energy intensity, and signal correlation features to quantitatively visualize structural damage. The effectiveness of the approach is demonstrated through the identification of various damage cases.

NO.3

Modified Lamb Wave Delay Factor-Based Multiply–Sum Probabilistic Diagnostic Imaging Algorithm of Composite Structures

Lingyu Sun , Juntao Wei , Chang Peng , Zengye Ju , Shanshan Lv , Faye Zhang , Lei Zhang , Lei Jia , and Mingshun Jiang

IEEE 2023


Review

  Probabilistic diagnostic imaging (PDI) method has been confirmed to be promising in structural health monitoring (SHM). The imaging capability of PDI method is limited by numerous factors, including the selection of probability distribution function and ellipsoidal scaling factor, the density of sensing arrays, and the damage index.
  The author proposed a modifed delay-factor-basde multiply-sum PDI (MDF-MSPDI) algorithm. In which the author obtains a more accurate delay factor (DF) by improving the culculation method of the group velocity of wave, and therefore the independence of gloabal accuracy on the selection of scaling factor β was reduced. At the same time, this study proposed an multiply–sum (MS) operation to reconstruct the damage imaging of each path, and generally reduce the dependence on individual paths, which can greatly improve the imaging accuracy of PDI method.

NO.4

Baseline-Free Damage Diagnostic Imaging Approach Relying on the Extraction of Converted Modes of Ultrasonic Guided Waves

Jiaqi Zhang, Hao Xu, Kai Zhou, Zhengyan Yang, Kehai Liu, Yuebin Zheng, Shuyi Ma, and Zhanjun Wu

ASCE 2021


Review

  A novel converted mode extraction-probabilistic diagnostic imaging (CME-PDI) method, which combined the conventional PDI and the CME methods, was proposed. By reconstructing the bisis mode, a new calculation method of damage index (DI) is presented, rusulting in the independence of the obtainment of baseline data, and therefore the CME-PDI method significantly improves the adapt ability of the traditional PDI method to the change of envirmental temperature.

NO.6

Multi-sensor network for industrial metal plate structure monitoring via time reversal ultrasonic guided wave

Zhang Hanfei, Cao Shuhao, Ma Shiwei, Lu Yu, Xiong Hanyu, Xia Qingwei, Liu Yanyan, Zhang Haiyan

Measurement 2020


Review

   The paper proposed a baseline-free time reversal probabilistic imaging (BFTRPI) algorithm for damage monitoring using Lamb waves, in which the time reversal principle was utilized to reconstruct structural response signals and eliminate the dispersion effect of Lamb waves. Therefore the algorithm could effectively identify multiple damages and significantly improve the accuracy of damage location compared with the traditional PDI method.

NO.7

A novel velocity anisotropy probability imaging method using ultrasonic guided waves for composite plates

Yuan Liu, Xiaobin Hong, Bin Zhang

Measurement 2020


Review

   The paper presented a novel velocity anisotropy probability imaging method for detecting damage in anisotropic composite plates, in which the velocity in all directions was calculated using numerical simulation techniques, the time coefficient and damage sensitive index were introduced for evaluating the probability of structural damage. Experimental results showed that the method accurately displays the damage information and could successfully detect the damage.

NO.8

Contact delamination detection of anisotropic composite plates using non-elliptical probability imaging of nonlinear ultrasonic guided waves

Yuan Liu, Xiaobin Hong, Bin Zhang

SHM 2022


Review

   In this paper, aiming at the contact delamination damage detection of anisotropic composite plates, a non-elliptical probability imaging (NEPI) method using a sparse sensor network based on nonlinear ultrasonic guided waves was proposed. The time coefficient (CT) and nonlinear damage index (NDI) based on smooth pseudo Wigner–Ville distribution (SPWVD) are defined and applied in the NEPI, which greatly improved the detection ability of the method for delamination damage compared with other damage detection methods.

NO.9

Probabilistic Diagnostic Algorithm‑Based Damage Detection for Plates with Non‑uniform Sections Using the Improved Weight Function

Jingjing Zhao, Xiaoting Miao, Fucai Li, and Hongguang Li

Journal of Vibration Engineering &Technologies 2018


Review

   The authors introduce a special method to calculate the time-of-flight (ToF) by dividing the plate into small grids and summing the distances between each grid. And therefore the use of the RAPID algorithm with a structural weight function for defect identification in non-uniform section plates (NUSPs) was proposed, which can considerably improve the precision of the localization, and the shape of the high-damage probability area, compared with the conventional RAPID.
